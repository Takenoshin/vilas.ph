The Context

In 2019, I was personally recruited by Austin Walper, a startup founder preparing to launch an esports engagement platform called PLAYR.gg. The company was still in its early seed stage, with no design foundation, no brand identity, and no product visuals — just a business idea and ambition.

Austin needed a design partner who could move fast, think strategically, and wear multiple hats across product and creative — and that’s where I came in.



Building from Zero to Hero

From day one, I took on a hybrid role as Founding Product & Creative Designer, working directly with the CEO. My responsibilities spanned multiple pillars:

Brand Identity Development

Created the visual identity from scratch and tone that helped position the brand as modern, credible, and esports-native.

Product UI/UX Design

Designed the web platform, focusing on user flows for brands and gamers, including campaign setup, engagement, and dashboards.

Client Facing Visual

Delivered tailored UI/UX solutions and visuals for bespoke branded activations with early clients and partners.

Pitch and Investor Decks

Delivered tailored UI/UX solutions and visuals for bespoke branded activations with early clients and partners.



The Trufan / Surf Acquisition

By 2020, the company was acquired by Trufan, later rebranded to Surf, validating the early design and business strategy we built. I remained involved post-acquisition, ensuring design continuity across evolving product needs.



The Outcomes

Helped the company secure $1 million in seed funding, with investor decks and product walkthroughs designed to impress and inform.

Delivered high-velocity design systems and user flows that enabled fast iteration, onboarding, and early client activation.

The company was successfully acquired by Trufan in 2020, then rebranded as Surf, cementing the product’s early market success.
