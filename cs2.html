Designing a Visual System for Gamer Giveaway Campaigns

  • 2019
  • UX Research
  • Visual Design


The Context

Surf Giveaways (formerly PLAYR.gg) started with a need for a clear and engaging promotional banner for a giveaway campaign. As more campaigns followed, the same structure proved consistently effective.

Through repeated application and refinement, the format evolved into a scalable visual system designed for clarity, speed, and adaptability. Over time, this system became a reference point and a trendsetter in gamer-focused visual marketing.



The Problem

Gamers scroll fast, and if the banner doesn't grab them in two seconds, it's gone.
Most campaigns lacked visual hooks strong enough to stop the scroll.
Lack of structure often results in low recognition of what’s actually being offered or who it’s from.


The Solution

DESIGNING FOR THE GAMER EYE

A purpose-built visual composition system was designed to match how gamers consume and scan content—prioritizing bold text hooks, gear-focused imagery, and clear brand alignment.

Dominant “GIVEAWAY” Text
Hero prize imagery as visual hook to stop the scroll.
Consistency in showing brands for trust


Woven tags can be removed and reattached to fakes.

Printed QR codes may fade or distort after washing.

Embedded NFC in Garment can melt during laundry



The Process

LEAN UX PROCESS

A Lean UX process was used to observe behavior, test ideas quickly, and shape a solution that delivers real user and business value through continuous iteration.

The Potential Impact

Up to 40% reduction in counterfeit-related issues in resale transactions

Up to 25% increase in buyer trust and purchase confidence

Stronger brand credibility in limited-edition releases and secondary markets

Higher perceived product value, supporting premium pricing and resale value
